Dinosaur History in Utah

Utah is home to a diverse wildlife and ecosystem, among the animals you can spot Mule Deer, Elk, Coyotes and even Mountain Lions. This gives exciting opportunities to view the wildlife animals and venture out in nature.

Where it all started and what dinosaurs once walked in Utah?
Utah holds precious fossils of late dinosaur remains, which helped provide valuable insight to the ecosystem over the millions of years. The states uniques geological formations eroding over time uncovers layers in the mudstone and rocks which lead to many great fossil discoveries and still hold footprints today.

Where can you find footprints on your adventure?
There are several locations across the state to view dinosaur footprints you can check out a few below:
  • Dinosaur National Monument: Located in northeastern Utah, this location has several spots to view fossils and tracks. The Dinosaur Quarry has a wall filled with dinosaur bones and if you look down you can find several footprints as well.
  • Moab, UT: Moab is home to Arches National Park and has a variety of locations to view dinosaur tracks along the Colorado river or in the sandstone dunes nearby.
  • Sand Dunes in San Rafael Swell: Sandstone is the perfect way that dinosaur tracts can still be preserved today. You can find footprints along the canyon at this location.
  • Antelope Island: Located in southern Utah, Antelope Island has very large dinosaur tracks and provides the opportunity to fossil hunt on your own during your adventure.
