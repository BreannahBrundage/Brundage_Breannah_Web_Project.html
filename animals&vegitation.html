Utah's Most Common Animals and Vegetation

Utah is home to a diverse wildlife and ecosystem, among the animals you can spot Mule Deer, Elk, Coyotes and even Mountain Lions. This gives exciting opportunities to view the wildlife animals and venture out in nature.

Animals
The most common animals include:
  • Mule Deer: Prefers mountainous regions and lower canyons. They stay huddled in small groups, and become active at dawn and dusk.
  • Elk: Often found in forested areas near national parks like the Rockys. Most popular hunted animal in Utah.
  • Coyotes: Very common throughout Utah, found in various habitats, including urban areas. They are normally nocturnal but can be seen during the day on occasion.
  • Mountain Lions: They are the big cats of the desert and prefer mountainous regions. They are most active around dawn and dusk and remain generally solitary except during mating season.
Vegetation
Common vegetation in Utah include:
  • Juniper Tree: One of the most common trees in Utah that provides shade from the desert heat and berries for the animals. The tree has twisted branches for bird and other wildlife to habitat.
  • Aspen Trees: Can be found in Utah and throughout Colorado, these beautify trees are famous for their incredible colorful fall foliage.
  • Desert Cacti: Can be found throughout Utah, so watch out! Cacti can produce beautiful flowers and even fruit.
  • Wildflowers: Flowers can be found around the spring and summer time, giving the area a splash of vibrant colors.
